The Caribbean Diving Encyclopedia

Your Definitive 2025 Guide to a Paradise in Transition

The Paradise Paradox

The Caribbean's coral reefs are at a historic inflection point. The dual impacts of Stony Coral Tissue Loss Disease (SCTLD) and the unprecedented 2023-2024 marine heatwave have reshaped underwater landscapes. Yet, this crisis has ignited a powerful, region-wide conservation movement. This guide is your compass to navigate this new reality, empowering you to become a force for regeneration while experiencing the profound beauty that remains.

Bonaire

The Shore Diving Capital & A World Leader in Reef Restoration

The Underwater Experience

  • Diving Freedom: Bonaire's hallmark is its unparalleled shore diving. Over 60 sites are marked by yellow stones. Rent a truck, grab tanks from a "drive-thru" station, and dive on your own schedule.
  • Signature Sites: Dive the iconic Hilma Hooker wreck, explore the coral-encrusted pillars of Salt Pier, or marvel at the pagoda-like star corals of 1000 Steps.
  • Klein Bonaire: A trip to this uninhabited islet is essential. Its 26 boat-only sites are exceptionally protected and often boast the island's best visibility and healthiest coral gardens.
  • Macro Life: A true hotspot for macro lovers. Search for elusive frogfish, seahorses, and colorful nudibranchs. Cleaner shrimp stations are abundant on nearly every reef.

The Ecological Story

Bonaire is a front-line battleground for reef survival. SCTLD was confirmed in March 2023, and the 2023 heatwave impacted over 80% of corals.

Status: Challenged but Resilient
  • Conservation Leadership: The Bonaire National Marine Park (BNMP), established in 1979, is a global model. All users purchase a "nature tag" to fund its management.
  • Reef Renewal Foundation Bonaire (RRFB): This world-class NGO is pioneering large-scale restoration. They manage vast staghorn and elkhorn coral nurseries and are at the forefront of collecting coral spawn to raise genetically diverse "super corals" in their lab.

Planning Your Trip

  • Buddy Dive Resort: The quintessential Bonaire experience. Famous for its drive-thru tank fills, on-site dive shop, multiple pools, and spacious apartments. Ideal for groups and independent divers.
  • Captain Don's Habitat: A historic resort founded by a diving legend. Offers a variety of accommodations, a PADI 5-star center, and a lively oceanfront bar.
  • Divi Flamingo Beach Resort: A more traditional resort with a casino, multiple restaurants, and a large fleet of dive boats for accessing Klein Bonaire and other sites.

Roatán, Honduras

The Vibrant, Diverse Heart of the Mesoamerican Reef

The Underwater Experience

  • Unmatched Variety: Roatán's location provides everything: sheer walls, intricate canyons, shallow reefs, and thrilling shark dives. The north and south shores offer different conditions for diving any day.
  • Signature Sites: Navigate the stunning volcanic fissures of Mary's Place, explore the 210-foot El Aguila Wreck, or experience the adrenaline of the Cara a Cara Shark Dive with its dozens of resident reef sharks.
  • Macro Life: The island is a haven for macro photographers, with abundant seahorses, pipefish, and nudibranchs for those with a keen eye. Look for the lettuce sea slug in shallow areas.

The Ecological Story

SCTLD arrived in September 2020, and the recent bleaching severely impacted key sites like the Cordelia Banks, a critical staghorn coral habitat.

Status: Stressed but Diverse
  • Community Power: The Roatán Marine Park (RMP) is a highly effective, community-led NGO. They actively patrol the reefs, manage coral nurseries, and run one of the most successful invasive lionfish control programs in the Caribbean.
  • Get Involved: Divers can become certified "RMP Coral Ambassadors" or Lionfish Hunters, directly participating in conservation during their vacation.

Planning Your Trip

  • Anthony's Key Resort: A classic, all-inclusive dive resort with charming over-the-water bungalows, an on-site dolphin research facility, and a massive, professional dive operation.
  • Mayan Princess Beach & Dive Resort: For those seeking luxury on the stunning West Bay Beach. Features upscale suites, a beautiful pool, and a full-service dive center on the sand.
  • Tranquilseas Eco Lodge & Dive Center: A boutique, sustainable option with beautiful hillside cabanas, a fantastic restaurant, and a highly personalized valet diving service.

Cayman Islands

A Story of Devastation, Resilience, and Astonishingly Clear Water

The Underwater Experience

  • Legendary Walls: The Caymans are defined by "devilishly deep drop-offs" and visibility often exceeding 100 feet. The wall diving is some of the most dramatic in the world.
  • Signature Sites: Dive the world-famous Bloody Bay Wall on Little Cayman. Explore all five decks of the massive USS Kittiwake wreck. And have a remarkable animal encounter at Stingray City in just 12 feet of water.
  • Three Islands: Grand Cayman offers accessibility and action. Little Cayman is a tranquil paradise. Cayman Brac offers a rugged, off-the-beaten-path feel with its own wreck, the MV Captain Keith Tibbetts.
  • Macro Life: Excellent for finding smaller creatures like Pederson cleaner shrimp, arrow crabs, and the fascinating secretary blenny peeking out from holes in the coral.

The Ecological Story

The 2023 heatwave was catastrophic. The Central Caribbean Marine Institute (CCMI) documented coral cover on Little Cayman plummeting from a 25-year high of 27% to an all-time low of 10%.

Status: Severely Impacted, Hopeful Recovery
  • A Lesson in Resilience: The coral loss led to an algae boom. Because the robust Marine Park system protected parrotfish, their populations exploded, and they are now clearing the algae, creating clean substrate for new corals to settle. It's a powerful demonstration that holistic ecosystem protection works.
  • Science-Driven: CCMI's 25-year monitoring program provides invaluable data, and they are now rebuilding their nurseries with "super corals" that survived the heatwave.

Planning Your Trip

  • Sunset House (Grand Cayman): A resort truly "by divers, for divers," with excellent shore diving on its house reef, an on-site dive shop, and a famous seaside bar.
  • Little Cayman Beach Resort: The premier base for diving Bloody Bay Wall. This intimate resort offers all-inclusive comfort and a renowned valet diving service.
  • Cobalt Coast Dive Resort (Grand Cayman): Located on the less-crowded North Shore, offering all-inclusive packages and easy access to the spectacular North Wall dives.

Cozumel, Mexico

The World Capital of Effortless, Exhilarating Drift Diving

The Underwater Experience

  • Drift Diving Mecca: The gentle current of the Mesoamerican Reef does the work for you, carrying you effortlessly past spectacular walls and coral formations.
  • Signature Sites: Fly along the thrilling Santa Rosa Wall, explore the mountainous coral pinnacles of the Palancar Reef System, and swim through clouds of fish at Paso del Cedral.
  • Endemic Treasure: Cozumel is the only place on Earth to find the rare and reclusive Splendid Toadfish, a beautiful bottom-dweller often found hiding under ledges.
  • Macro Life: While known for big scenery, the reefs are full of smaller life. Look for arrow crabs on sponges and various species of shrimp and blennies hiding in crevices.

The Ecological Story

SCTLD hit Cozumel early and hard in 2018, causing a devastating 46% loss in live coral cover and downgrading its reef health index from "Good" to "Fair."

Status: Significantly Impacted, Critical Recovery
  • Resting the Reefs: The National Marine Park authority (CONANP) has implemented a crucial rotational closure program, resting key reef systems like Palancar and Colombia for several months each year to allow for undisturbed recovery and coral spawning.
  • Community Restoration: The Cozumel Coral Reef Restoration Program (CCRRP) is a vital community-led non-profit that has outplanted over 10,000 coral fragments and offers courses for visiting divers.

Planning Your Trip

  • Secrets Aura Cozumel: An adults-only, all-inclusive luxury option with a full-service, on-site dive operator (Dive Palancar), multiple pools, and premium dining.
  • Scuba Club Cozumel: A classic, dedicated dive resort where everything is built around the dive schedule. Simple, comfortable rooms and a fantastic on-site operation with private piers.
  • The Explorean Cozumel: An all-inclusive that combines diving with other adventures, offering daily guided expeditions like jungle tours and kayaking alongside diving access.

Belize

The Wild Frontier of Atolls and the Great Blue Hole

The Underwater Experience

  • The Great Blue Hole: A true bucket-list dive. Descend into a massive, 400-foot-deep marine sinkhole to see ancient, submerged stalactites. It's a dive about geology and the thrill of the deep.
  • Remote Atolls: Belize's best diving is on its three remote atolls: Turneffe, Lighthouse, and Glover's Reef. These protected areas offer pristine walls and abundant marine life, best accessed by liveaboard.
  • Whale Sharks: At Gladden Spit, encounter the ocean's largest fish as they congregate to feed on snapper spawn around the full moon (April-June).
  • Macro Life: The atolls are great for spotting colorful Flamingo Tongue snails on sea fans, Pederson shrimp, and a wide variety of crabs and blennies in the healthy reef structures.

The Ecological Story

While the overall reef health is rated "poor" by the Mesoamerican Reef Report Card, Belize's fish populations have surged thanks to effective protections within its vast marine reserves.

Status: Variable, Strong Protections
  • UNESCO Heritage: The Belize Barrier Reef Reserve System (BBRRS) is a network of seven protected areas recognized for its global significance.
  • Blue Bond Initiative: A groundbreaking debt-for-nature swap is funding a massive expansion of Belize's "no-take" zones, tripling the area of full protection and showing a major commitment to recovery.

Planning Your Trip

  • Belize Aggressor III & IV (Liveaboards): The most efficient way to dive the Blue Hole and remote atolls, offering multiple dives per day, comfortable cabins, and chef-prepared meals.
  • Turneffe Island Resort: A private island resort offering upscale villas, personalized service, and access to over 70 dive sites on the Turneffe Atoll.
  • Hamanasi Adventure & Dive Resort: A mainland eco-resort that offers both reef and rainforest adventures, with daily boat trips to the southern barrier reef.

Cancún, Mexico

A Unique Realm of Underwater Art and Mystical Caverns

The Underwater Experience

  • MUSA (Underwater Museum): A truly unique dive within a submerged art park of over 500 life-sized sculptures. This artificial reef project is a surreal and beautiful intersection of art and conservation.
  • Gateway to Cenotes: Cancún is the primary hub for accessing the Yucatán's world-famous cenotes. Experience the magic of diving in crystal-clear freshwater caverns with stunning light effects and geological formations. Day trips to sites like Dos Ojos and The Pit are easily arranged.
  • Wrecks & Reefs: Dive the C-58 Minesweeper wreck, a 184-foot vessel split in two by a hurricane, which attracts eagle rays and large schools of fish.
  • Macro Life: While not a primary macro destination, the reefs and wrecks host arrow crabs, banded coral shrimp, and blennies. The MUSA sculptures are also becoming habitats for smaller, colonizing creatures.

The Ecological Story

Cancún's natural reefs are heavily impacted by tourism and SCTLD. The conservation story here is one of innovation and diverting pressure from natural ecosystems.

Status: Unique Artificial Reefs & Stressed Natural Reefs
  • Art as a Solution: MUSA was specifically created to provide a compelling alternative dive site, drawing thousands of divers away from the fragile natural reefs and creating a new habitat for marine life to colonize.
  • Cenote Conservation: The primary rule for cenote diving is to protect the pristine water and delicate formations. This means no sunscreen or bug spray, and absolutely no touching the stalactites or stalagmites.

Planning Your Trip

  • Nizuc Resort & Spa: A top-tier luxury resort in a more secluded area. Offers sophisticated style, multiple pools, a world-class spa, and can arrange high-end, private dive and cenote tours.
  • Live Aqua Beach Resort Cancún: An adults-only, all-inclusive focused on sensory experiences. Features multiple pools, aromatherapy, and easy access to dive shop connections for MUSA and cenote trips.
  • Fiesta Americana Condesa Cancún: A great family-friendly all-inclusive with a large, lagoon-style pool and an on-site water sports and dive center, making logistics simple.

Dive with Purpose

The future of the Caribbean's reefs depends on the choices we make today. By supporting sustainable operators, mastering your dive skills, and participating in citizen science, you become a vital ally in the fight to preserve this fragile paradise for generations to come.
